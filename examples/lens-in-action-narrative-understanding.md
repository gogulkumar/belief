# Lens In Action — Narrative Understanding

## The Document (Same Quarterly Review As Business Memory Example, Cycle 4)

> "Q3 revenue came in at $2.1B, up 7% year-over-year, with growth
> across all three reported segments. Product A contributed $1.4B
> (67% of total), Services $0.5B (24%), and Platform $0.2B (9%).
> Operating profit was $380M, with Product A generating $340M of
> that total.
>
> Headcount grew 11% in the period, driven by engineering and
> product hiring in support of the Platform roadmap. Platform
> revenue remains early-stage.
>
> Cost per unit in the Services segment increased 8% year-over-year,
> with management attributing this to investment in delivery capacity
> ahead of anticipated volume growth."

**Prior context for this example:**
In Cycle 3 (prior quarterly review), the Services segment was described
as "on track for margin improvement in H2." In Cycle 2 (the annual
report), Services was described as "a growing profit contributor."

---

## The Reasoning

**Reading this through the Narrative Understanding lens.**

The narrative lens does not re-read the numbers. It reads the language
relative to the numbers and relative to prior documents.

**Observation 1: Services language has shifted**
Cycle 2: "a growing profit contributor"
Cycle 3: "on track for margin improvement in H2"
Cycle 4: no forward profitability claim for Services at all; instead,
"cost per unit increased 8%, investment ahead of anticipated volume growth"

This is a specific, observable shift. Services went from being
described as a profit contributor, to being on track for improvement,
to having a cost increase explained away as investment. No profitability
claim appears in Cycle 4.

— Is this a narrative divergence? Yes — the language is softening in
  correlation with the Services numbers (24% of revenue, 10% of profit
  based on math from BM-01).
— Is it one observation? Across three documents, the language has moved
  consistently in one direction: fewer forward profitability claims as
  the actual profitability story gets weaker.
— Falsifiable? Yes. A future document could restore specific Services
  profitability claims if margins improve.
— One observation or a pattern? This is the third document in the
  sequence — the direction has been consistent. Seed at 0.20.

**→ SEED NC-04**: "Forward profitability language about the Services
segment has softened across three documents, moving from explicit profit
contributor framing to cost-explanation language as the segment's actual
profit contribution has declined relative to revenue share."
Confidence 0.20. Evidence count 1 (pattern across 3 docs counted as
first observation because it's one directional trend). Direction:
Deteriorating.

**Observation 2: Platform described as "early-stage" with no forward target**
The Platform roadmap is cited as the reason for 11% headcount growth.
But no target for Platform revenue, no timeline for when "early-stage"
ends, no metric for how success will be measured.

In Cycle 2, the annual report included a "Platform milestone" section
with a specific revenue target for Year 3. That section is absent in
Cycle 4.

— Observable anchor: a specific section with a specific metric present
  in Cycle 2, absent in Cycle 4.
— Is the absence explained? No. No divestiture, no program end, no
  structural reason. The milestone section simply does not appear.
— Could the Cycle 2 target have been met? No — Platform revenue is 9%
  of total, and the Cycle 2 target was substantially higher.
— Falsifiable? Yes. A future document could restore a Platform revenue
  target or milestone framework.

**→ SEED NC-05**: "A Platform revenue milestone section present in the
annual report has disappeared from subsequent quarterly documents as
Platform revenue remains well below the implied target — forward
visibility into Platform is being reduced, not expanded."
Confidence 0.20. Evidence count 1. Direction: Deteriorating.

**Observation 3: "Growth across all three reported segments"**
The opening line claims growth across all segments. Product A grew.
Services grew in revenue. Platform grew. This is technically accurate.
But the narrative lens asks: is the framing consistent with the
economic story? Product A is 89% of profit. Saying "growth across all
three segments" frames a structurally concentrated business as a
diversified one.

— Is this a narrative divergence? Potentially — but this framing has
  been consistent across all four documents. It is not a new divergence,
  it is a stable framing choice.
— Already held? Check world model: BM-01 (profit concentration) is a
  business memory belief at 0.74. The narrative framing that contradicts
  it is part of the context for that belief, not a new narrative belief.

**→ SILENT.** This framing is already captured in the business memory
belief about profit concentration. Adding a separate narrative belief
about the same gap would be redundant. The existing BM-01 holds the
interpretation.

## What This Example Shows Alongside The Business Memory Example

The same document produced:
- **Business Memory lens**: 1 update (BM-01), 1 seed (BM-05), 2 silences
- **Narrative lens**: 2 new seeds (NC-04, NC-05), 1 silence

**They read the same text and produced different beliefs.** This is
correct. The lenses are not redundant — they extract different types
of interpretation from the same raw material. Business memory asked
what is structurally true about how the business is built. Narrative
understanding asked how the language is changing relative to the
underlying reality.

The Services cost increase was SILENT in the business memory lens
(one period, plausible attribution) but became a NARRATIVE belief
(three documents of softening language showing a clear directional
pattern). The same evidence, read through a different lens, produces
a different type of understanding.
