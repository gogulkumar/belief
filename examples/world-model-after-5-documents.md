# World Model — After 5 Documents

**Business:** GenericCo (illustrative)
**Documents read:** Annual report, two quarterly reviews, an internal
strategy presentation, a headcount and budget planning document
**Date of this snapshot:** Cycle 5

This is what the world model looks like after five documents. Most
observations from those five documents are not here. They are filed
in the archive as facts. Only interpretations that are durable,
falsifiable, and specific to this business are held as beliefs.

## BUSINESS MEMORY

**BM-01**
The business generates the majority of its profit from one product
line even though it presents itself as a diversified portfolio.
The secondary product lines contribute revenue but operate near
breakeven.
Confidence: 0.74 | Direction: Stable | Evidence count: 4
Last updated: Cycle 4 (confirmed)
*Could be falsified by: secondary lines becoming meaningfully profitable*

**BM-02**
Revenue growth is driven almost entirely by price increases rather
than volume expansion. Unit volumes have been flat or declining for
three periods while average revenue per unit has risen each period.
Confidence: 0.62 | Direction: Deteriorating | Evidence count: 3
Last updated: Cycle 5 (confirmed)
*Could be falsified by: volume recovering while pricing stabilizes*

**BM-03**
The cost structure does not improve as the business grows. Cost per
unit of output has risen in four of the five periods observed, even
as total output grew — the business is not gaining efficiency from
scale.
Confidence: 0.58 | Direction: Deteriorating | Evidence count: 3
Last updated: Cycle 5 (confirmed)
*Could be falsified by: unit costs declining as volume grows*

**BM-04** [UNCERTAIN — held at floor confidence]
A transformation program announced as a top priority has not received
the headcount or budget visible in the operating plan. Resources have
flowed to near-term commercial activity instead.
Confidence: 0.28 | Direction: Unclear | Evidence count: 2
Last updated: Cycle 4 (seeded Cycle 3)
*Needs one more confirming document before it rises above 0.35*
*Could be falsified by: operating plan showing dedicated transformation budget*

## BUSINESS DYNAMICS

**BD-01**
Headcount growth in support functions precedes revenue softness by
approximately one period — this relationship has appeared in two of
the three periods where it was visible.
Confidence: 0.36 | Direction: Unclear | Evidence count: 2
Last updated: Cycle 4
*Two observations is not enough to treat this as reliable. Tracking.*
*Could be falsified by: support headcount growing without subsequent
revenue softness in the following period*

**BD-02**
Customer retention rates in one period translate into higher revenue
per customer approximately two periods later. The relationship has
held across three cycles.
Confidence: 0.52 | Direction: Improving | Evidence count: 3
Last updated: Cycle 5 (confirmed)
*Could be falsified by: retention improving without subsequent revenue
per customer expansion*

## NARRATIVE CAPTURING

**NC-01**
Revenue misses are attributed to external timing factors, but the same
demand pattern recurs the following period. The external timing
framing has appeared in three of four underperformance periods with
no equivalent attribution in the two outperformance periods.
Confidence: 0.61 | Direction: Deteriorating | Evidence count: 4
Last updated: Cycle 5 (confirmed)
*Could be falsified by: external attribution appearing in an
outperformance period, or demand recovering after being attributed
to timing*

**NC-02**
Annual targets are set and then revised downward mid-year. In each of
the three years observed, the year-end result was below the original
commitment and the following year's guidance was set below the
original target without a recovery path.
Confidence: 0.69 | Direction: Deteriorating | Evidence count: 3
Last updated: Cycle 5 (confirmed)
*Could be falsified by: targets held through the year without revision*

**NC-03** [SEEDED — one observation only]
A performance metric that appeared prominently in four consecutive
documents is absent from the most recent document, in a period where
results were below plan.
Confidence: 0.20 | Direction: Unclear | Evidence count: 1
Last updated: Cycle 5 (seeded)
*Holding. One observation. Needs to recur before rising.*
*Could be falsified by: metric restored in next document regardless
of performance*

## FACTUAL UNDERSTANDING (Tracked Signals)

The following have been observed but have not yet formed beliefs.
They are tracked here as signals awaiting pattern confirmation.

**FU-T01** — Operating margin has been flat for five periods despite
management commentary describing "efficiency gains." No belief yet —
the gap between commentary and result is consistent with NC-01 but
has not been independently confirmed as a pattern specific to margin.
Filed: Cycles 1–5.

**FU-T02** — One key market showed an unusual revenue composition
shift in Cycle 4. Revenue from existing customers declined while new
customer revenue rose — the opposite of the company-level pattern.
Anomaly noted. One observation. No belief formed. Watching Cycle 6.

**FU-T03** — Support headcount grew 22% in Cycle 2. Revenue declined
in Cycle 3. See BD-01 — this is the first data point of that
relationship, not yet enough to confirm.
