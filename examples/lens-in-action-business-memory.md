# Lens In Action — Business Memory

## The Document (Quarterly Review, Cycle 4)

> "Q3 revenue came in at $2.1B, up 7% year-over-year, with growth
> across all three reported segments. Product A contributed $1.4B
> (67% of total), Services $0.5B (24%), and Platform $0.2B (9%).
> Operating profit was $380M, with Product A generating $340M of
> that total.
>
> Headcount grew 11% in the period, driven by engineering and
> product hiring in support of the Platform roadmap. Platform
> revenue remains early-stage.
>
> Cost per unit in the Services segment increased 8% year-over-year,
> with management attributing this to investment in delivery capacity
> ahead of anticipated volume growth."

---

## The Reasoning

**Reading this through the Business Memory lens.**

First, what is this document revealing about the structure of the
business — things that would still be true in a year?

**Observation 1: Profit concentration**
Revenue is 67% Product A. Operating profit is $340M of $380M — that
is 89% from one segment that is 67% of revenue. The business is more
dependent on Product A in profit terms than the revenue headline suggests.

— Falsifiable? Yes. A future document could show Services or Platform
  becoming meaningfully profitable, reducing Product A's share.
— Distinctive? Yes. The gap between revenue share (67%) and profit
  share (89%) is specific to how this business's cost structure sits
  across its segments.
— Durable? Yes. This is not a one-quarter result — it reveals the
  underlying segment economics.
— Already held? Check world model: BM-01 exists at confidence 0.66,
  evidence count 3. This confirms it.

**→ UPDATE BM-01**: Confidence 0.66 → 0.74. Evidence count 3 → 4.
Direction: Stable (the gap has not widened or narrowed materially).

**Observation 2: Platform as early-stage with no profit**
Platform is 9% of revenue and generating negligible profit given
the total operating profit math. With headcount growing 11% partly
attributed to Platform investment, the cost is being absorbed in
central overhead rather than in the Platform segment's own P&L.

— Falsifiable? Yes. A future document could show Platform reaching
  meaningful profitability.
— Distinctive? The specific decision to invest in Platform while
  absorbing its cost centrally rather than segment-attributing it
  is a structural choice specific to this business.
— Is this one belief or two? It is one: the structural choice of
  how Platform investment is being accounted for and what it means
  for the profit concentration story.
— Already held? No.

**→ SEED BM-05**: "Platform investment is being absorbed into central
overhead rather than attributed to the Platform segment — this
understates the true drag on Product A's standalone economics."
Confidence 0.20. Evidence count 1. Direction: Unclear.

**Observation 3: Services cost per unit rising**
Services cost per unit rose 8%, attributed to investment ahead of
volume. One period, plausible explanation, non-recurring attribution.

— Is this a structural belief? Not yet. One period, management
  attribution is plausible (investment ahead of volume is a real
  phenomenon). This does not meet the distinctiveness test yet —
  any services business investing in capacity would show this.

**→ SILENT.** File the Services unit cost increase in Factual
Understanding as a signal to track (FU-T04). Watch Cycle 5 for
whether volume materialized to justify the investment.

**Observation 4: Revenue growth 7%, headcount growth 11%**
Headcount growing faster than revenue in one period.

— Structural belief? Not from one period. Could be investment cycle,
  restructure, hiring timing. Fails durability test as a single
  observation.

**→ SILENT.** Note the ratio in Factual Understanding. If this
relationship repeats in Cycle 5, it may become a Business Memory
belief about organizational scaling efficiency.
