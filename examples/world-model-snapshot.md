## BUSINESS MEMORY

**BM-01** — Profit concentration
The business generates 89% of operating profit from Product A, which
represents 67% of revenue. The profit-to-revenue ratio gap has been
stable for four observed periods — the business is more concentrated
than the headline revenue mix suggests.
Confidence: 0.82 | Direction: Stable | Evidence: 5
Last updated: Cycle 5 (confirmed)

**BM-02** — Growth engine: price not volume
Revenue growth has been driven by price increases for three consecutive
periods. Unit volumes in the core segment have been flat or declining.
The business is monetizing its existing base, not expanding it.
Confidence: 0.66 | Direction: Deteriorating | Evidence: 3
Last updated: Cycle 5 (confirmed)

**BM-03** — Cost structure does not improve with scale
Cost per unit of output has risen in four of five observed periods
despite total output growing. The organization is scaling linearly,
not gaining efficiency from growth.
Confidence: 0.58 | Direction: Deteriorating | Evidence: 3
Last updated: Cycle 5 (confirmed)

**BM-04** — Strategic priority underfunded
A transformation program described as a top priority in the strategy
presentation has not received dedicated headcount or budget in two
subsequent operating reviews. Resources have flowed to near-term
commercial activity instead.
Confidence: 0.36 | Direction: Deteriorating | Evidence: 2
Last updated: Cycle 5 (confirmed — weakly)
*NOTE: Still below 0.40. Requires one more confirming document.*

**BM-05** — Platform cost absorption
Platform investment is being absorbed into central overhead rather
than attributed to the Platform segment. This understates the true
drag on Product A's standalone economics.
Confidence: 0.28 | Direction: Unclear | Evidence: 1
Last updated: Cycle 4 (seeded)
*NOTE: Single observation. Tracking.*

## CAUSAL UNDERSTANDING

**CU-01** — Onboarding completion leads churn
A meaningful decline in onboarding completion rate precedes a churn
increase by approximately one to two periods. Observed twice in the
declining direction. Q4 recovery in onboarding predicted churn
normalization in H1 next year — pending confirmation in Cycle 6.
Confidence: 0.28 | Direction: Unclear | Evidence: 2
Watch item: Cycle 6 churn data will test the improving direction.

**CU-02** — Platform headcount leads Platform revenue (hypothesis only)
Not yet a belief. Platform headcount has grown for three periods.
Platform revenue has not followed. If Platform revenue does not respond
by Cycle 7, this may become a belief about disconnection between
investment and output. Currently tracked in Factual Understanding (FU-C02).
