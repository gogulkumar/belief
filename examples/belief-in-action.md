# Belief In Action

One document. Three beliefs formed. Everything else silent.

---

## The Document (Quarterly Review, Cycle 4)

> "Q3 revenue came in at $2.1B, up 7% year-over-year, with growth
> across all three reported segments. Product A contributed $1.4B
> (67% of total), Services $0.5B (24%), and Platform $0.2B (9%).
> Operating profit was $380M, with Product A generating $340M of
> that total.
>
> Headcount grew 11% in the period, driven by engineering and product
> hiring in support of the Platform roadmap. Platform revenue remains
> early-stage.
>
> Cost per unit in the Services segment increased 8% year-over-year,
> with management attributing this to investment in delivery capacity
> ahead of anticipated volume growth.
>
> In prior documents, Services was described as 'a growing profit
> contributor' and 'on track for margin improvement in H2.' No such
> claim appears in this document."

---

## What The Belief System Does With This

### Belief BM-01 — Updated (Business Memory)

Existing belief: "The business generates the majority of its profit
from Product A even though revenue appears more diversified."
(Confidence 0.66, evidence 3)

Product A is 67% of revenue but 89% of operating profit ($340M of
$380M). The gap between revenue share and profit share has now
appeared for the fourth consecutive period.

Falsifiable — future document could show Services or Platform becoming
meaningfully profitable. Distinctive — the specific gap between revenue
mix and profit mix is specific to this business's segment economics.
Pattern confirmed.

**→ Confidence 0.66 → 0.74. Evidence 3 → 4. Direction: Stable.**

---

### Belief BM-05 — Seeded (Business Memory)

Platform investment is being absorbed into central overhead (11%
headcount growth "in support of the Platform roadmap") rather than
attributed to the Platform segment's own P&L. This understates the
drag on Product A's standalone economics.

Falsifiable — a future document could show Platform costs segment-
attributed or Platform reaching profitability. Distinctive — the
specific accounting treatment of Platform investment is a structural
choice specific to this business.

First observation. Cannot confirm a pattern yet but the observation
is clear enough to hold.

**→ Seeded at 0.20. Evidence 1. Direction: Unclear.**

---

### Belief NU-04 — Seeded (Narrative)

In Cycles 2 and 3, Services was described with explicit forward
profitability language: "growing profit contributor," "on track for
margin improvement in H2." In Cycle 4, those claims have disappeared
and been replaced with cost explanation language, in a period where
Services profit is visibly thin ($40M on $500M revenue = 8% margin
vs Product A at 24%).

Observable anchor: specific language present in prior documents,
absent in this one, in correlation with a weaker result. Falsifiable
— a future document could restore Services profitability claims if
margin improves.

**→ Seeded at 0.20. Evidence 1. Direction: Deteriorating.**

---

## What Was Filed Silently

**Services cost per unit +8%** — one period, plausible seasonal
attribution (investment ahead of volume). No pattern established.
Filed to factual understanding tracker. Watching next period.

**Headcount 11% vs revenue 7%** — one period. Could be investment
cycle, hiring timing, restructure. Not durable from one observation.
Filed to factual understanding tracker.

**"Growth across all three segments"** — technically accurate but
frames a concentrated business as diversified. Already captured in
BM-01. No separate belief needed — that would be duplication.
