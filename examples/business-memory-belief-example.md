# Business Memory Belief — TechCorp Inc.

*Last updated: Q3 2025 | Documents read: 3 earnings transcripts*

---

### Profit Concentration

Product generates approximately 90% of operating profit while
representing two-thirds of revenue — and this concentration is
increasing as the business grows, not decreasing. Services and
Platform contribute revenue but remain economically thin. TechCorp
describes itself as a diversified portfolio. In profit terms it is
not.

Confidence: 0.66 | Direction: Deteriorating

---

### Growth Engine

Revenue growth is driven by pricing, not by volume or customer
expansion. Customer count grows in low single digits each quarter.
Volume is flat. Price increases are doing the work. The business
is monetizing its existing base rather than expanding it, and the
growth rate is decelerating as that lever compresses.

Confidence: 0.66 | Direction: Deteriorating

---

### Cost Structure

The organization does not gain efficiency as it grows. Headcount
has grown faster than revenue for three consecutive quarters. Cost
per unit has risen each period. Scale is not producing leverage —
the business is expanding linearly, adding cost in proportion to
or faster than output.

Confidence: 0.59 | Direction: Deteriorating

---

### Segment Economics

Platform investment costs are absorbed into central overhead rather
than attributed to the Platform segment. This makes Product's
standalone economics appear stronger than they are and makes
Platform's drag invisible in segment reporting. Services shows
growing revenue share but margin improvement described as "still
building" with no figure attached across three transcripts.

Confidence: 0.44 | Direction: Unclear

---

**What would revise this belief:** A quarter where Services or
Platform contributes meaningfully to profit, where volume or new
customer growth drives revenue rather than pricing, or where the
headcount-to-revenue ratio improves as the business grows.
